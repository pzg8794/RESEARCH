I am applying for the doctoral position in Machine Learning for Reliable Quantum Computing at KTH. The project's focus on quantum noise, error mitigation, and scalable models for reliable quantum computation is especially compelling to me because my current graduate research already centers on reliability-oriented machine learning for quantum settings, with emphasis on disciplined experimentation, structured evaluation, and decision-making under stochastic and adversarial conditions.

Through these projects, I have learned how to reason carefully about noisy measurements, sparse feedback, changing environments, and the difference between apparent performance in a narrow setup and genuine robustness across conditions.

What attracts me most in this KTH project is the opportunity to move from reliability questions in quantum networking and resource allocation toward reliability questions closer to the core of quantum computation itself. I am especially interested in learning how machine learning methods can be constrained by physical structure, used efficiently with limited data, and made trustworthy enough to support quantum error mitigation and related reliability tasks. My present work has given me a strong foundation in Python-based experimentation, algorithm comparison, and reproducible evaluation; I would be excited to extend that foundation toward physics-informed and structured learning for quantum noise processes and reliable quantum algorithms.

KTH is a particularly strong match because this project combines deep technical focus with a broader research environment that values both methodological rigor and long-term impact. The connection to the WASP Graduate School, the Department of Computational Science and Technology, and an international research community makes this a compelling place to grow as a doctoral researcher. I am looking for a PhD environment where strong machine learning work is tied directly to significant quantum-computing challenges, and where careful evaluation, collaboration, and research depth are treated as central rather than secondary.

I would be excited to contribute to KTH's research community and to develop as a doctoral researcher working on machine learning for reliable quantum computing. Thank you for considering my application.
